SONYC: Tutorial

Welcome to New York University's sound tagging application. Please read this tutorial carefully before proceeding.

Upon completing this tutorial you will be presented with a form. You must fill out this form before proceeding.

Filling out the form:
At the top of the form page you will find a personl code. Please copy and paste this code into the first box of the form:

After pasting your personal code, please complete the remainder of the form and click the "submit" button:

Once you've submitted the form, a "Continue" button will appear to proceed to the application.

Your goal:
In this app, we ask you to tag (i.e. to label) the different sources of sound that you can hear in a sound clip. Examples of sound sources are "car horn", "dog bark", "siren", etc. Your goal is to label all the sounds you can hear in the provided sound clip.

Listening to the sound clip:
We will present you with a sound clip, visualized as a spectrogram. Beneath the spectrogram you will find the Play and Rewind buttons that allow you to listen to the sound as many times as you like. There is also a Show Tutorial button that allows you to go back to this tutorial at any time:

Tagging sound sources: Step 1
After listening to the sound clip, the next step is to tag all the sound sources you've heard. For example, let's say you heard a car horn. The first step is to select a salience level, i.e. is the car horn in the foreground or background? If you're not sure, you can select not sure:

Tagging sound sources: Steps 2-3
After selecting the salience of the sound source, you need to indicate the source itself. You can do so by clicking on one of the provided tags (in our example we'd click on car horn):

If the source you heard is not one of the provided tags, you can create a custom tag instead:

Tagging sound sources: Step 4
